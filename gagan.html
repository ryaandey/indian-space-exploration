GAGANYAAN: INDIA'S FIRST HUMAN SPACEFLIGHT MISSION

Gaganyaan is India’s first planned human spaceflight mission, developed by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
It aims to send Indian astronauts, or "vyomanauts," into low Earth orbit, marking a major milestone in India's space program.

Key Facts:

Mission Objective: The goal of Gaganyaan is to demonstrate India’s capability for human spaceflight by launching astronauts into space and safely returning them to Earth.
This includes testing life-support systems, spacecraft design, and mission planning.

Mission Timeline: Originally scheduled for 2022, the mission timeline has been adjusted to ensure safety and precision, with unmanned test missions planned before the crewed flight.

Spacecraft Design: The Gaganyaan spacecraft includes a crew module for astronauts and a service module for propulsion and life-support systems.
It is designed to carry a crew of up to three astronauts for a mission lasting approximately 5 to 7 days.

Training and Collaboration: ISRO has partnered with Russia for astronaut training, with selected Indian astronauts undergoing extensive training in Russia and India to prepare for the mission.

Significance:

Gaganyaan will make India the fourth country to send humans into space, joining the ranks of the United States, Russia, and China.
This mission will enhance India's capabilities in human spaceflight and open doors for future manned missions, including potential missions to the Moon.
