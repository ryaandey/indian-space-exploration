MANGALYAAN: INDIA'S MARS ORBITER MISSION

Mangalyaan, also known as the Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM), is India's first interplanetary mission, launched by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
It marked India’s entry into deep space exploration and showcased its ability to carry out complex space missions on a modest budget.

Key Facts:

Launch Date: Mangalyaan was launched on November 5, 2013, using the PSLV-C25 launch vehicle.

Objective: The mission aimed to explore Mars’ surface, morphology, atmosphere, and mineralogy from orbit.
It also sought to demonstrate India’s capability for interplanetary exploration.

Historic Achievement: On September 24, 2014, Mangalyaan successfully entered Mars orbit on its first attempt.
This made India the first country to reach Mars on its maiden mission and the fourth space agency globally to do so.

Cost-Effectiveness: Mangalyaan is known for its remarkable cost-effectiveness, with a mission budget of approximately $74 million, one of the lowest for a Mars mission.

Significance:

Mangalyaan has provided valuable data on Mars’ surface features, atmospheric conditions, and mineral composition.
The mission's success showcased ISRO’s growing capabilities in space science and established India as a key player in planetary exploration.
