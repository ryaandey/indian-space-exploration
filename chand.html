CHANDRAYAAN: INDIA’S LUNAR EXPLORATION PROGRAM

Chandrayaan is India’s series of lunar exploration missions developed by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
The program aims to explore the Moon's surface and gather valuable scientific data.

Key Missions:

- Chandrayaan-1: Launched on October 22, 2008, it was India's first mission to the Moon.
Chandrayaan-1 successfully orbited the Moon and made significant discoveries, including the presence of water molecules on the lunar surface.

- Chandrayaan-2: Launched on July 22, 2019, this mission included an orbiter, a lander (Vikram), and a rover (Pragyan).
While the orbiter continues to study the Moon from above, the lander unfortunately lost communication during its descent.

- Chandrayaan-3: Launched on July 14, 2023, this mission aimed to achieve a soft landing on the Moon.
Chandrayaan-3 successfully landed on August 23, 2023, near the lunar south pole, making India the fourth country to achieve this feat.

Significance:

The Chandrayaan missions have significantly advanced lunar science and increased our understanding of the Moon’s geology and environment.
These missions have also enhanced India’s reputation in the global space community and paved the way for future explorations, including potential manned missions to the Moon.
