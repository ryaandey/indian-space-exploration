INDIAN SPACE EXPLORATION: A JOUNEY THROUGH SPACE

Indian space exploration has grown from humble beginnings to an inspiring journey marked by remarkable achievements.
Managed by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), India's space program began in 1962 with a mission to use space technology for national development.
Over the years, it has evolved to achieve world-class milestones, showcasing the country’s prowess in science and engineering.

Starting with the launch of its first satellite, Aryabhata, in 1975, ISRO has continuously expanded its capabilities.
India’s space program has achieved a reputation for innovative, cost-effective space missions, becoming the first nation to reach Mars on its maiden attempt with the Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan) in 2014.
Additionally, ISRO’s Chandrayaan missions have contributed valuable insights about the Moon, and Chandrayaan-3 recently achieved a historic soft landing near the lunar south pole in 2023, making India the fourth country in the world to achieve this feat.

Today, India is also exploring planetary missions, human spaceflight programs (Gaganyaan), and ambitious plans for interplanetary exploration.
By combining indigenous research with collaborative international efforts, Indian space exploration continues to inspire and propel the nation toward new frontiers in space science and discovery.



What is ISRO?

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is India’s national space agency, headquartered in Bengaluru, India.
Established in 1969, ISRO aims to harness space technology for national development and advancements in space science.
ISRO has been responsible for developing satellite and launch vehicle technology, focusing on cost-effective and innovative missions.
Its notable achievements include the Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan), which made India the first country to reach Mars on its maiden attempt, and the Chandrayaan missions that have provided significant insights into lunar science.
ISRO is also working on future projects, such as the Gaganyaan human spaceflight mission, to further expand India’s space exploration capabilities.
